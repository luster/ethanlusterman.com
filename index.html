I am a part-time software engineer at The Hackerati, located in New York, NY. During my time here, I have worked on a number of projets, including:

  • maintaining the APIs and editorial CMS for a comScore Top 50 website (PHP, JS, HTML5, CSS3)
  • designing a REST API (Python/Django)
  • r&d for a machine learning system to replace an outdated retail market research company's categorization system (AWS, Python, MATLAB)
  • managing and designing an app for making stock portfolio decisions using machine learning and historical financial data as well as Twitter and News data

Many people have asked me over the past few years if I could make websites for their bands or personal portfolios, so I've decided to take on some part-time projects. The first site I built is for the band Time King. I worked with my friends in the band as well as with graphic artist Louis Costa. The site is static and built on GitHub pages from scratch. I also have experience putting sites into production on Heroku and DigitalOcean using frameworks such as Django and Flask.

I graduated from The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art with a B.E. in Electrical Engineering this past May, and I am currently pursuing an M.E. in Electrical Engineering. My research is focused on the intersection of machine learning, signal processing, and music. During my time at Cooper Union, I have worked on projects in music genre classification and clustering, audio dereverberation, music transcription, digital guitar effects generation, and psychoacoustic compression of audio, among others.

In my free time, I like to write, play, and record music as well as go to shows. I released my first EP, Better Off, with help from a few friends this past year. I have performed at venues and festivals such as Bowery Electric, Lit Lounge, The Vibe Lounge, and Vans Warped Tour.
