I am a part-time software engineer at The Hackerati, located in New York, NY. I worked on a team that maintained the APIs and editorial CMS for a Top 50 website. I have also worked on a RESTful user API in Django, meant to serve as a starting point for new applications that need to support users. Most recently, I have been working on a big data/machine learning project for a top market research company using tools such as Hadoop, Pig, MATLAB, and Python.

I graduated from The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art with a B.E. in Electrical Engineering this past May, and I am currently pursuing an M.E. in Electrical Engineering. My research is focused on the intersection of machine learning, signal processing, and music. During my time at Cooper Union, I have worked on projects in music genre classification and clustering, audio dereverberation, music transcription, digital guitar effects generation, and psychoacoustic compression of audio, among others.

In my free time, I like to write, play, and record music as well as go to shows. I started playing guitar and singing when I was 12. I self-released my first EP, Better Off, with help from a few friends this past year. I have performed at venues and festivals such as Bowery Electric, Lit Lounge, The Vibe Lounge, and Vans Warped Tour. This summer, I'll be working on a lengthier release and booking more shows!
